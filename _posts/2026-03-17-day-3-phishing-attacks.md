## 🚨 If it feels urgent, it’s probably a scam.

Phishing attacks are one of the most common and dangerous cyber threats today. Attackers pretend to be trusted organizations—like banks, social media platforms, or even your workplace—to trick you into revealing sensitive information.

These attacks usually come in the form of emails, SMS, or fake websites that look almost identical to legitimate ones. Messages like *“Verify your account now”* or *“Your account will be suspended!”* are designed to create panic and force you into quick action.

Once you click a malicious link or enter your credentials, attackers can gain access to your accounts, financial data, or even your identity.

## 🔍 How to Protect Yourself

- Always verify the sender’s email address  
- Hover over links before clicking  
- Check for spelling mistakes and suspicious URLs  
- Never share passwords or OTPs through messages  
- Avoid acting on urgent or threatening messages  

## ⚠️ Key Takeaways

- Phishing relies on **trust and urgency**  
- Fake emails and websites look very real  
- One click can compromise your entire account  
- Awareness is your first line of defense  

## 🛡️ Stay Secure

Cybersecurity is not just for experts—it’s for everyone. The more aware you are, the harder it becomes for attackers to succeed.

Stay alert. Think before you click.
