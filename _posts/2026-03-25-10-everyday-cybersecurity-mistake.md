# 10 Everyday Cybersecurity Mistakes (You Probably Make)

You don’t need to be a hacker to get hacked.

You just need to make a few small mistakes…

👉 The same ones millions of people make every day.

---

## ⚠️ Why This Matters

Most cyber attacks don’t happen because of advanced hacking.

They happen because of **simple everyday mistakes**.

Here are 10 you might be making right now:

---

## 🔟 The Mistakes

### 1. Reusing Passwords  
One password across multiple accounts = one breach, everything gone.

---

### 2. Clicking Links Without Thinking  
If it creates urgency or fear… it’s probably a trap.

---

### 3. Using Public Wi-Fi Without Protection  
Free Wi-Fi = open door for attackers.

---

### 4. Ignoring Software Updates  
Updates fix security flaws. Skipping them = staying vulnerable.

---

### 5. Weak or Predictable Passwords  
“123456” or your birthday? Easy targets.

---

### 6. Not Using 2FA  
One extra step can block most attacks—but many still ignore it.

---

### 7. Oversharing on Social Media  
Your personal info = hacker’s research data.

---

### 8. Downloading Unknown Files  
That “free” file could cost you everything.

---

### 9. Trusting Every Email or Message  
Even if it looks real… always verify.

---

### 10. Saving Passwords Everywhere  
Browsers, notes, random apps—risky if your device is compromised.

---

## 🛡️ What You Should Do

Start small:

- Use unique passwords  
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)  
- Avoid suspicious links  
- Keep your software updated  

👉 **Cybersecurity is not a tool. It’s a habit.**

---

## 🚀 Final Thought

You don’t need to make all 10 mistakes.

👉 Just one is enough.

Hackers don’t always break systems.

They wait for you to make a mistake.
