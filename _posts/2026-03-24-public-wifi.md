# Public Wi-Fi: Your Data’s Open Door

Free Wi-Fi feels convenient.

At a café.  
At an airport.  
At a hotel.  

You connect… and continue your work.

But what if I tell you…

👉 **You might not be the only one on that connection.**

---

## 💀 The Hidden Risk of Public Wi-Fi

Public networks are often:

- Unsecured  
- Poorly configured  
- Easily accessible  

Which makes them perfect for attackers.

👉 **It’s like sending your data through an open street.**

Anyone can intercept it.

---

## 🧠 What Happens When You Connect

When you join public Wi-Fi:

- Your device communicates openly  
- Data travels across shared networks  
- Security depends on the network setup  

And if it’s not secure…

👉 Your data can be captured.

---

## 🕵️‍♂️ The Most Common Attack: Man-in-the-Middle

In this attack:

- A hacker positions themselves between you and the network  
- They intercept your data  
- They can even modify what you see  

👉 You think you’re browsing safely…

But everything passes through them.

---

## 🎯 Fake Wi-Fi Networks (Evil Twin Attack)

This is even more dangerous.

Hackers create a network like:

> “Free Airport WiFi”  
> “CoffeeShop Guest”

You connect.

But it’s not real.

👉 It’s controlled by the attacker.

---

## 💀 A Real Scenario

You connect to café Wi-Fi.

You log into your email.

Everything seems normal.

But in the background:

- Your login data is captured  
- Your session is hijacked  
- Your account is compromised  

👉 And you don’t even notice.

---

## ⚠️ What Hackers Can Steal

On public Wi-Fi, attackers can access:

- Login credentials  
- Emails  
- Browsing activity  
- Personal data  

Especially if the connection is not encrypted.

---

## 🛡️ How to Stay Safe

You don’t need to avoid public Wi-Fi completely.

But you must use it carefully:

- Avoid logging into sensitive accounts  
- Use HTTPS websites only  
- Turn off auto-connect  
- Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network)  
- Forget networks after use  

Most importantly:

👉 **Treat public Wi-Fi as untrusted. Always.**

---

## 🚀 Final Thought

Free Wi-Fi is never truly free.

You might not pay with money…

👉 But you could be paying with your data.
